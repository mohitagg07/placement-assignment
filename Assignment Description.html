Assignment Overview

You are tasked with implementing a 2D rectangular packing algorithm to solve a spatial placement problem. This assignment focuses on constraint-based optimization and geometric algorithms for placing rectangular components on a circuit board.

Background Context: In circuit board design, components must be placed on a rectangular board following specific spatial rules. Your algorithm will determine optimal positions for rectangular components while satisfying placement constraints.

Important Notice: Hardcoding component positions to specific coordinates is strictly prohibited and will result in assignment failure. Your solution must use algorithmic approaches to determine component placement based on the constraints provided.

Problem Definition

Input Specifications

Board Dimensions:

  • 2D rectangular grid: 50 × 50 units
  • Origin at top-left corner (0,0)
  • Coordinate system: (x, y) where x increases rightward, y increases downward
  • Grid Constraint: All component coordinates are on a 1-unit grid.

Components to Place:

  1. USB_CONNECTOR - Size: 5 × 5 units - Must be placed along any board edge
  2. MICROCONTROLLER - Size: 5 × 5 units - Can be placed anywhere (subject to proximity constraint)
  3. MIKROBUS_CONNECTOR_1 - Size: 5 × 15 units - Must be placed along a board edge
  4. MIKROBUS_CONNECTOR_2 - Size: 5 × 15 units - Must be placed along a board edge
  5. CRYSTAL - Size: 5 × 5 units - Must be placed near the MICROCONTROLLER

Orientation Constraint: Each component may be placed with rotation 0° or 90°.

Constraint Requirements

Hard Constraints (Must be satisfied)

  1. Edge Placement: USB_CONNECTOR, MIKROBUS_CONNECTOR_1, and MIKROBUS_CONNECTOR_2 must have at least one edge touching the board boundary
  2. Parallel Placement: MIKROBUS_CONNECTOR_1 and MIKROBUS_CONNECTOR_2 must be:
    • Placed on opposite edges of the board
    • Aligned parallel to each other (same orientation)
  3. Proximity Constraint: CRYSTAL must be placed within maximum distance of 10 units from MICROCONTROLLER center
  4. No Overlapping: Components cannot overlap with each other
  5. Boundary Constraint: All components must be completely within board boundaries
  6. Global Balance Constraint: The geometric center of mass of all 5 placed components must lie within a 2.0-unit radius of the exact center of the board (25, 25). The center of mass is the average of the center coordinates of all components.
  7. Crystal Keep-Out Zone: The connection between the CRYSTAL and MICROCONTROLLER is a clock signal path that is sensitive to noise from the USB_CONNECTOR.
    • Define a rectangular "keep-out zone" that is 10 units wide and extends 15 units directly inward from the center of the USB_CONNECTOR's edge on the board.
    • The straight line segment connecting the center of the CRYSTAL to the center of the MICROCONTROLLER is forbidden from intersecting this keep-out zone.

Soft Constraints (Optimization goals)

  1. Minimize wasted space
  2. Maximize compactness of overall layout
  3. Prefer center placement for non-constrained components

Technical Requirements

Algorithm Implementation

You must develop an algorithmic solution to solve this placement problem. Your implementation should determine optimal component positions while satisfying all constraints.

Placement Strategy

Your implementation should determine component positions algorithmically. Consider developing scoring functions that balance space efficiency with constraint satisfaction.

Constraint Integration Strategy

  3. Validation Phase: Verify all constraints after placement
